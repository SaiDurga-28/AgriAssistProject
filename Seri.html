Detailed Weather Impact Analysis

Heatwave Impact on Silkworm Rearing

When temperatures exceed 30°C, silkworms face thermal stress, leading to reduced appetite, slower growth, and lower cocoon quality. Extreme heat during rearing can reduce cocoon yield and increase larval mortality significantly.

Key Precautions:

  • Install ventilation and cooling systems in rearing houses
  • Use wet gunny cloths or foggers to regulate humidity
  • Ensure sufficient hydration and fresh mulberry leaves
  • Rearing during early morning or late evening hours

Winter Cold Effects on Silkworm Rearing

Cold temperatures below 15°C slow down silkworm metabolism, reducing feeding activity and delaying cocoon formation. Prolonged exposure to cold can lead to poor cocoon quality and increased larval mortality if not managed effectively.

Essential Measures:

  • Maintain optimal room temperature with heaters
  • Cover rearing trays with protective cloths
  • Prevent drafts by sealing rearing house gaps
  • Ensure fresh, warm mulberry leaves are provided

Monsoon Challenges in Sericulture

Excessive rainfall increases humidity levels, which creates a favorable environment for fungal and bacterial infections in silkworms. Wet conditions can also degrade mulberry leaf quality, affecting silkworm health and cocoon development.

Prevention Strategies:

  • Ensure proper ventilation in rearing houses
  • Dry and disinfect rearing spaces regularly
  • Harvest and store mulberry leaves properly
  • Control humidity with dehumidifiers or lime

Extreme Weather Impact on Sericulture

Cyclones, floods, and storms can damage rearing houses, destroy mulberry plantations, and disrupt silkworm life cycles. Quick response and preparedness are crucial to protect silkworms and ensure continuity of cocoon production.

Emergency Actions:

  • Reinforce silkworm rearing houses
  • Store dry mulberry leaves for backup feeding
  • Relocate worms to safe, dry areas during floods
  • Maintain backup power and ventilation systems

Weather Solutions Guide – Sericulture

Weather Challenge Recommended Solution
Extreme Heat (>30°C)
Install cooling systems in rearing houses, avoid mid-day feeding, and apply mulches to reduce mulberry leaf stress.
Severe Cold (<15°C)
Use heaters or warm lamps in rearing rooms, insulate silkworm trays, and adjust humidity levels accordingly.
Heavy Rainfall
Ensure proper drainage in mulberry fields, prevent leaf fungal infections, and maintain dry and clean rearing space.
High Humidity
Control room humidity using ventilation and lime trays, monitor for grasserie and muscardine diseases in silkworms.
Cyclones/Storms
Reinforce rearing houses, harvest mature cocoons early, and ensure backup power for climate control equipment.
Temperature Fluctuations
Use automated temperature sensors, maintain consistent indoor climate, and adjust feeding intervals accordingly.
