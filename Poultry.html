Detailed Weather Impact Analysis

Heatwave Impact on Poultry Birds

When temperatures rise above 30°C, poultry birds suffer from heat stress, leading to reduced feed intake, lower egg production, slower weight gain, and in severe cases, increased mortality. Heat stress can cause up to 25% drop in productivity during peak summer.

Key Precautions:

  • Ensure proper ventilation and airflow in coops
  • Use cooling pads, fans, or foggers
  • Maintain continuous access to clean drinking water
  • Feed birds during cooler hours of the day

Cold Weather Impact on Poultry Birds

Temperatures below 10°C can stress poultry birds, increasing their energy needs to maintain body heat. This leads to reduced feed efficiency, slower growth rates, and lower egg production, especially in poorly insulated housing.

Essential Measures:

  • Provide well-insulated and draft-free shelters
  • Offer high-energy feed formulations
  • Ensure water doesn't freeze and remains accessible
  • Use heat lamps or bedding for warmth

Rainy Season Challenges in Poultry Farming

Excessive rainfall and high humidity create wet litter conditions, promoting fungal and bacterial growth. This leads to respiratory infections, footpad dermatitis, and increased mortality in poultry flocks.

Prevention Strategies:

  • Ensure proper drainage and roofing
  • Use absorbent, dry bedding materials
  • Maintain good ventilation to reduce humidity
  • Follow strict hygiene and biosecurity protocols

Cyclone and Flood Impact on Poultry Farms

Severe storms, cyclones, and floods can destroy poultry sheds, disrupt feed and water supply, and cause high mortality due to drowning or stress. Proper infrastructure and emergency planning are critical to protect poultry flocks.

Emergency Actions:

  • Elevate poultry sheds above flood level
  • Stock emergency feed and clean water
  • Strengthen roofing and walls to resist winds
  • Train staff for disaster response

Weather Solutions Guide

Weather Challenge Recommended Solution
Extreme Heat (>30°C)
Install cooling fans and misters, provide shade, increase water availability
Severe Cold (<10°C)
Use heat lamps, provide insulated shelters, increase energy-rich feed
Heavy Rainfall
Ensure proper drainage, keep bedding dry, maintain ventilation
High Humidity
Improve ventilation, use dehumidifiers if possible, monitor respiratory health
Cyclones/Storms
Develop evacuation plans, secure poultry houses, stock emergency feed and water
Temperature Fluctuations
Monitor weather forecasts, adjust feeding and lighting schedules, use climate control
